# Brainwave Entrainment and Cognitive Performance
This project investigates whether brainwave entrainment (BWE) can influence cognitive performance, using experimental methods and statistical analysis.

The study was conducted as part of a course in AI and Human Thinking at the University of Gothenburg.

## Overview
Brainwave entrainment uses auditory and visual stimuli to influence brainwave activity. It has been proposed as a method for enhancing cognitive functions such as attention and creativity.

This study evaluates whether such effects can be observed in controlled cognitive tasks.

## Method
Participants (N = 10) completed two tasks before and after exposure to BWE stimuli:
- Alternative Uses Task (AUT) — measuring creative thinking
- Continuous Performance Task (CPT) — measuring attention and response control

Additional analysis explored whether belief in BWE correlated with performance changes.

Statistical methods included:
- Wilcoxon signed-rank test (pre/post comparison)
- Mann-Whitney U test (group comparison)
- Spearman correlation (belief vs performance change)

## Results
No statistically significant effects were found for:
- AUT performance (creativity)
- CPT accuracy
- CPT reaction time

No significant correlation was observed between belief in BWE and performance changes.

Small directional effects were observed in some metrics, but with high uncertainty.

## Interpretation
The results do not support BWE as a reliable method for cognitive enhancement in this study.

However, limitations — particularly small sample size (N = 10) — introduce substantial uncertainty.

The study highlights the importance of:
- careful statistical interpretation
- skepticism toward claimed cognitive enhancement methods
- the role of experimental design in evaluating AI-related technologies
